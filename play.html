The Grim Latinx Fairy Tale Cycle

The trilogy starts with The River Bride and also includes Wolf at the Door and Alcira.

"Inspired by Latina/o mythology and fairy tales from Europe, the plays in this cycle are cautionary tales for adults to help us navigate our emotional lives. Like the Brothers Grimm's fairy tales, these plays are dark, violent, and love is complicated." - Orta

Orta says that "writing The River Bride I found myself returning to my roots as a poet. Of all my plays it has the most heightened language...my poetics are very present in my playwriting -- lyricism, imagery, metaphor. And I consider myself an Imagist -- inspired by and using strong imagery as part of my narratives." - Orta

Portuguese Pronunciations

Character Names

  • Costa - A very common Portuguese surname, particularly in Brazil; meaning coast (close to the sea/water).
  • Helena - A Latin form of the name "Helen", of Greek origin, meaning "bright", "shining light", or "ray of light".
  • Belmira - "Beautiful Vision," or as Moises says, "beautiful to look upon."
  • Duarte - A common Portuguese name, a version of Edward or Eduardo; these names mean wealth and protector, a wealthy or prosperous guardian/protector.
  • Moises Lira - Lira means "balance" and is a common Portuguese surname. Moises is a common male first name in Portuguese, and a version of "Moses" which in some interpretations means "child of the Nile" or the river, often linked to being drawn from the water.

Themes, Symbols, and Motifs

  • Poetic language for emotional affective response
  • Desire vs duty
  • Sisterhood, jealousy, and striving towards freedom
  • Water as transformative, water as a boundary or limen
  • Magical Realism - "Originating in Latin America, magical realism combines elements of fantasy fiction with mundane details of reality...much of magical realism is about folklore, mythology, and fairy tales" and "to me, magical realism often reads the same way my most bizarre dreams feel. Not scary, but not not scary, and a reality that is more of an unreality but one that I trust while I'm in the dream."
  • Orchid (Cattleye Labiata) - A brazillian-native orchid that varies in color and size; can symbolise resilience, endurance, strenght, beauty, and love.
